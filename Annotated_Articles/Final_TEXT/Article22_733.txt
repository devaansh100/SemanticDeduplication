
['Six fined for sharing meat of spotted deer near Coimbatore\nCOIMBATORE: Forest department officials from the Periyanaickenpalayam range imposed a fine of Rs 90,000 on six farm workers on Saturday for sharing the meat of a spotted deer which was killed by a stray dog at Jamukandi area near Anaikatti.A male spotted deer was stuck in a fence installed around a piece of patta land after a stray dog chased the deer at Jambukandi near Anaikatti in Periyanaickenpalayam forest range on Saturday. The dog killed the spotted deer and started to eat the meat. Meanwhile, six farm workers who were working on patta land, chased the stray dog away and shared the meat of the spotted deer.Kopanari beat forester Mathusoothanan, forest guard Kumar, Annamalai and anti-poaching watchers got a tip-off and rushed to the area where they caught the farm workers red-handed. The forest department officials seized the spotted deer meat.They imposed a fine of Rs 15,000 each on farm workers S Murugan, 48, his wife M Sivakami, 38, M Shanthi, 37, S Valli, 29, S Sindhu, 28 and S Sivakami, 48. All hailed from Alamaramedu at Anaikatti in Coimbatore district. The officials collected Rs 90,000 as fine amount from them.']
