
["Female tiger cub found dead in Madhya Pradesh’s Bandhavgarh Reserve; death toll rises to 15 in four months\nRepresentational Image Photo : IANS Madhya Pradesh : The carcass of a female tiger cub was recovered from inside the : The carcass of a female tiger cub was recovered from inside the Bandhavgarh Tiger Reserve (BTR) in Umaria district of Madhya Pradesh on Sunday evening, said the forest officials on Monday. With this, Madhya Pradesh has lost 15 tigers in the last four months in different wildlife habitats of the state, including six in less than two weeks. The carcass of the tigress was spotted by a patrolling team near Dhamokhar buffer area at compartment number 126. The female tiger cub was around eight to nine months old, reported the TOI. The patrolling team informed the matter to the senior officers and the field director. The officers informed that the animal had multiple injury marks on its body, including in skull, abdomen, limbs and ears. The forest officials suspect that the tigress was killed by an adult male during a territorial fight, added the TOI report. The officials told that the samples were preserved after post-mortem and the carcass of the animal was disposed of as per National Tiger Conservation Authority 's (NTCA) norms. As many as 11 tigers have died within the tiger reserves while four were reported dead outside the area, including one in a road mishap, added the TOI report. As per a data revealed by the All-India Tiger Estimation Report 2018, Madhya Pradesh has a population of 526 tigers, the highest in any state of the country."]
