
['Andhra Pradesh: 13 member gang involved in red sand boas smuggling arrested in Chittoor district\nTIRUPATI: A thirteen member interstate gang involved in the illegal trade of red sand boas were arrested by the police and the forest department officials in a joint operation on Tuesday.According to DFO GG Narendran, local police and the forest department staffers acted on specific information and conducted raids at Chennamma Gudipalli village in Chittoor district and apprehended the 13 member gang who belonged to the four southern states of Andhra Pradesh , Tamil Nadu, Kerala and Karnataka.In the preliminary investigation, it was revealed that the prime accused M Gopal of Ottuvaripalli, Vedamur mandal in Chittoor district tried to sell red sand boas, while Kannan Pandurangan of Tamil Nadu came to buy the red sand boa and KK Hiran of Kerala and C Raghava Reddy of Karnataka acted as middlemen in the illegal trade.Sand boas are used in making certain medicines and in the fashion industry and in performing of black magic.Besides arresting the accused, the team also rescued the reptile. An SUV and a Royal Enfield bike, one Suzuki access scooter along with 13 cell phones have been seized. Those arrested have been identified as M Gopal (38), Kannan Pandurangan (42), KK Hiran (27), V Govindappa (48), C Raghava Reddy (48), TS Sarath Sashidhar (32), K Arshad Ali (28), N Salman (30), M Kadhir (24), M Kumar (31), G Siva Sankar (26), B Usman (21) and S Siddiq (20).']
