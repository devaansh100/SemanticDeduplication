
['Coimbatore forest officials arrest two men for poaching monitor lizards\nTwo people were arrested for poaching three monitor lizards in the Tholamapalayan east beat of Periyanaickenpalayam forest range by the Coimbatore forest division officials. According to a report published in the Times of India (TOI), the accused, identified as Rajendra (41) and Mani (42) hail from Samakkal Nagar in Bilichi Goundanur village. The report suggests that the duo was spotted by a team spearheaded by K Muthu from Thadagam reserve when they were making rounds of the forest on the evening of October 5. The team found that both the accused were involved in poaching of monitor lizards and tried to run without taking the carcasses of lizards and snares. Later on, the carcasses of monitor lizards and snares were seized by the forest department officials. Quoting sources, TOI reported that the accused duo were interrogated by Periyanaickenpalayam forest range officer S Selvaraj and they reportedly confessed to their crime. A sickle was also seized from them. The duo were produced before a judicial magistrate in the court at Mettupalayam on Thursday and are currently in prison. Meanwhile, a veterinary doctor with the Kovanur veterinary hospital performed an autopsy and the carcasses of the monitor lizards were buried inside the forest reserve.
