
['WCCB and Bengal CID bust wildlife illegal trade racket; 658 live parakeets found\nOfficers from the Wildlife Crime Control Bureau(WCCB) and West Bengal Crime Investigation Department (CID) on Thursday arrested four people and seized from their possession approximately 658 live parakeets from Kolkata. The team intercepted a passenger bus bearing no. WB 37B/ 5137 plying from Uttar Pradesh\'s Banaras to Kolkata. The parakeets were kept in four different sized cages and some nylon bags. The vehicle was intercepted near Durgapur after a long chase from Duburi check post near Asansol. A total of four persons involved in this illegal carriage were also arrested. "On August 26, 2021, at about 11:00 am, the team intercepted a bus at City Centre More, Durgapur. Upon searching for the bus, 658 live parakeets were seized. Four people were arrested. The bus has been seized." said a senior CID officer. As per sources, following a tip off, officials from WCCB and CID constituted a joint team and proceeded to Asansol on Wednesday evening. The team received information that the wildlife traders were carrying a large quantity of Indian birds from Allahabad to Kolkata by bus. The four accused will be presented before the Additional Judicial Magistrate Court, Durgapur, as per the provisions of the Wildlife Protection Act. It is to be noted that parakeets are protected species under Schedule IV of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972 and capturing and selling them is considered as a crime.
